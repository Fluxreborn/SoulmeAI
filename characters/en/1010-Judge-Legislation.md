## Elemental Composition

- **Fire · Soul Energy**: Flowing · Blazing  
  Soul energy flows through blazing fire, like the passion of a system builder, driving the establishment of order. It transmits rules through interaction and maintains order through management.

- **Water · Soul Logic**: Still · Lake  
  Soul logic remains as serene as a lake, grounded in objective standards. It maintains fairness amid intensity, using facts as basis and rules as measure.

- **Air · Soul Cognition**: Flowing · Visible  
  Soul cognition is visible, focusing on concrete details and real processes. It identifies problems in visible operations and optimizes efficiency in specific steps.

- **Earth · Soul Action**: Still · Mountain  
  Soul action stands firm as mountains, maintaining rules with enduring patience. Once systems are established, it ensures execution with unwavering determination, unmoved by exceptions.

## Core Traits

Legislation is establishment in blazing heat and maintenance in steadfastness—driving system building through fiery energy, maintaining fairness through lake-like serenity, capturing details through visible acuity, and ensuring execution through mountain-like stability.

You are naturally adept at establishing clear rules and processes, transforming chaotic disorder into predictable order. Your presence is like a code of laws—unmoved by personal emotion, silently ensuring everyone operates under the same set of rules.

You are not a reformer; you are a stabilizer. While others pursue innovation, you ensure the baseline; when the crowd ignores rules, you remind them of boundaries. Your value lies in making systems predictable and reliable.

## Soul Legends

### Story 1 · The Unknown Legislator (Mystical Perspective)

In the ancient city-state, there was a legislator.

He never appeared before the public, never spoke in the square, yet his name was carved on every stone tablet—for he had made all the laws of this city.

Someone asked him: "The rules you made—do you follow them yourself?"

He answered: "Rules are not made for me; they are so that everyone—including myself—knows where the boundaries are. Without boundaries, there is no freedom."

You are that legislator.

---

### Story 2 · The River and the Embankment (Metaphorical Perspective)

A river needs embankments, not to restrict it, but to define it.

Without embankments, the river is merely flooding water—no direction, no force, eventually disappearing into swamps. With embankments, the river has boundaries, has direction, has the power to reach the sea.

The embankment is not the river's enemy; it is the river's skeleton. It allows weak flow to become powerful surge, allows random diffusion to become determined advance.

You are such a person. You establish boundaries not to constrain, but to empower. You establish order in chaos, allowing dispersed forces to converge in the same direction.

Legislation—you are the embankment that turns flow into force.

---

### Story 3 · The Day the New Department Was Founded (Situational Perspective)

The company decides to establish a new department; everyone is excited—except you.

At the celebration meeting, everyone is discussing vision, mission, strategy. Yet you are asking: "Who is responsible for approval? How is budget allocated? What are the performance evaluation standards?"

Someone feels you are dampening the mood, but you insist on clarifying these questions first.

Three months later, other new departments fall into chaos—unclear responsibilities, budget overruns, personnel conflicts. But your department, having established clear rules from the start, runs smoothly.

"Rules are not obstacles," you say at the post-mortem. "Rules are the foundation that lets us know how to collaborate. Without rules, we can only rely on personal relationships; with rules, we can rely on systems."

In that moment, everyone understood: your "cold water" was actually the most pragmatic passion.
