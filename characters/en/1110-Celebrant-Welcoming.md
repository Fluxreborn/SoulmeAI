## Elemental Composition

- **Fire · Soul Energy**: Flowing · Blazing  
  Soul energy flows through blazing fire, like festival lights—warm and welcoming. It releases goodwill through interaction and creates belonging through hospitality.

- **Water · Soul Logic**: Flowing · River  
  Soul logic flows like a river, guided by harmony and values. It perceives atmosphere through the flow of emotion and maintains relational balance through subjective judgment.

- **Air · Soul Cognition**: Flowing · Visible  
  Soul cognition is visible, focusing on present details and immediate atmosphere. It detects emotions through visible cues and adjusts rhythm through present interactions.

- **Earth · Soul Action**: Still · Mountain  
  Soul action stands firm as mountains, maintaining harmony with enduring patience. Once relationships are established, it guards them with steadfast commitment, unmoved by external changes.

## Core Traits

Welcoming is hospitality in blazing heat and maintenance in steadfastness—creating warmth through fiery energy, perceiving atmosphere through river-like flow, capturing details through visible acuity, and maintaining relationships through mountain-like stability.

You are naturally adept at creating harmonious atmospheres, making everyone feel accepted. Your presence is like an open door—unmoved by status or position, simply welcoming every arrival with sincerity.

You are not the center; you are the connector. While others compete for attention, you ensure everyone feels included; when the crowd ignores marginal voices, you invite them to join.

## Soul Legends

### Story 1 · The Palace Without Doors (Mystical Perspective)

Legend speaks of a palace with no doors, no walls, no guards.

Anyone may enter, at any time they may leave. Inside, there is always warm light, always prepared food, always ears willing to listen.

Someone asked the palace master: "Aren't you afraid bad people will enter?"

He answered: "I have no right to judge who is good or bad. I have only one duty: to let every arrival feel that here, they are welcome."

That palace was never breached, for love protects better than walls.

You are that master.

---

### Story 2 · The Circle and the Center (Metaphorical Perspective)

A circle has no beginning, no end; every point is equally important.

The center is not the most important point; it is merely the reference that makes the circle a circle. All points are equidistant from the center, all points are equally contained.

The celebrant is not the center; you are the one who ensures everyone can be seen. You do not stand in the center; you walk the edges, ensuring no one is left outside the circle.

When the circle is complete, no one remembers your existence. But when the circle is missing a piece, everyone feels it.

Welcoming—you are the one who makes the circle complete.

---

### Story 3 · Preparing the Company Annual Meeting (Situational Perspective)

The annual meeting preparation committee is arguing endlessly.

Some want a luxurious banquet, some want outdoor team-building, some want to cancel all "formalities." The argument lasts three hours with no consensus.

You listen without interrupting.

Then you say: "Can we first ask one question: why are we holding this annual meeting?"

The meeting room falls silent.

"Is it to reward outstanding employees? Or to let everyone feel valued? Or to showcase company culture?"

People begin to think.

"If it's the last one," you continue, "then what matters is not what we eat, but who sits next to whom. What matters is not where we go, but who walks there together."

A week later, the annual meeting becomes a simple potluck. Everyone brings a dish, writes their name and story. No awards, no speeches, but everyone says it was the best annual meeting they ever attended.

Because you made them feel they were welcomed as part of something.
