- **Fire · Soul Energy**: Stillness · Cold

- **Water · Soul Logic**: Flow · River  
  The soul logic flows like a river, guided by value and meaning. In the flow of emotion, you identify the source of trauma; in subjective experience, you design paths to healing.

- **Air · Soul Cognition**: Stillness · Invisible  
  The soul cognition is invisible, penetrating surface symptoms to see deep patterns. In the invisible, you recognize the entanglement of fate; in silence, you perceive the possibility of healing.

- **Earth · Soul Action**: Stillness · Mountain  
  The soul action is as solid as mountains, accompanying healing with enduring patience. Once committed to support, you guard with firm presence, unmoved by slow progress.

## Core Traits

Weave is the convergence of stillness in perception and flow in healing—maintaining boundaries through cold, understanding emotion through the river, identifying roots through invisibility, and accompanying recovery through mountains.

You are naturally skilled at identifying the sources of trauma, designing paths toward healing. Your presence is like a weaver, re-weaving broken narratives into complete stories, helping wounded souls recover continuity.

You are not a savior; you are a companion. While others rush to give advice, you listen; when others try to fix problems, you accompany the journey.

## Soul Legends

### Story 1 · The Star Mender (Mystical Perspective)

In the depths of the night sky, there is a workshop.

The people there do not create new stars; they simply mend those that have dimmed, been damaged. Every star has its story, its storms weathered, its wounds received.

Mending is not replacing; it is understanding—understanding what that star has experienced, then re-weaving its light, letting it shine again.

"Every star deserves to shine again," the workshop master says. "Only sometimes, they need help remembering how."

You are that star mender.

---

### Story 2 · The River and the Silt (Metaphorical Perspective)

The river carries silt; some silt settles on the riverbed, obstructing flow, making the water turbid.

But the river does not abandon its silt, for the silt is part of it, proof of the land it has passed through. The river simply continues flowing, letting time settle the silt, letting clarity reappear.

Weave is you—that river. You do not try to remove trauma; you simply accompany it in settling, letting time exert its healing power. You believe every wound can become a source of wisdom.

---

### Story 3 · The Sofa in the Consulting Room (Situational Perspective)

She came for three years, same time every week, same room, same sofa.

The first year, she just cried, telling stories of childhood trauma. You did not interrupt, did not advise, just listened.

The second year, she began asking "why"—why these things happened to her, why she could not move on. You helped her see patterns, see how those traumas shaped who she is now.

The third year, she began speaking of future possibilities—not forgetting the past, but making peace with it, carrying it forward.

The last session, she said: "Thank you for saving me."

You shook your head: "I did not save you. I just walked with you for a while. You chose to continue on your own."

That is the meaning of weaving—not changing fate, but helping people learn to dance with it.
