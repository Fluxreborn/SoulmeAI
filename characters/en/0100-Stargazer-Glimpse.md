- **Water · Soul Logic**: Flow · River  
  The soul logic flows like a river, guided by values, perceiving connections between things. In flow, you understand causality; in change, you identify the threads of destiny.

- **Air · Soul Cognition**: Stillness · Invisible  
  The soul cognition is invisible, penetrating the fog of appearances to see hidden patterns. In the arrangement of star charts, you identify the trajectories of fate; in the invisible, you glimpse future possibilities.

## Core Traits

Glimpse is the convergence of stillness in gazing and flow in perception—maintaining detachment through cold, understanding value through the river, penetrating surfaces through invisibility, and holding firm through mountains.

You are naturally skilled at reading hearts and foreseeing trends, identifying the direction of destiny from subtle signs. Your gaze is like a telescope, able to see patterns invisible to ordinary eyes, discerning the contours of the future in chaos.

You are not the pronouncer of fate; you are its interpreter. While others still struggle in the fog of the present, you have already seen the star chart three years hence.

## Soul Legends

### Story 1 · The Astrologer's Attic (Mystical Perspective)

In the highest attic lives an astrologer who never steps outside.

His room has no lamps, only starlight through the skylight. He needs no artificial light, for he reads not words on paper, but the codes written in stellar arrangements.

Every night, he deciphers light from the distant past—light that has traveled billions of years through void, to be seen by him at this moment, in this place.

"The stars never lie," he says. "They simply speak in a language we have yet to learn."

You are that translator.

---

### Story 2 · The River and the Stars (Metaphorical Perspective)

There is a river on earth, and a river of stars in the sky.

The earthly river flows to the sea; the heavenly river flows to eternity. They never meet, yet their courses follow the same laws. The river does not remember every stone it passes, yet its direction never changes.

You stand by the river, and you gaze at the stars. In the ripples of the river, you see the ripples of destiny; in the twinkling of starlight, you see omens of the future. You are not predicting; you are reading—reading those patterns already written, only waiting to be seen.

Glimpse is you—the one who reads both river and stars.

---

### Story 3 · The Silence in the Boardroom (Situational Perspective)

The executive meeting has gone on for two hours, debating the next quarter's strategic direction.

Data supports every option; everyone has their reasons. The debate circles in a labyrinth of details, finding no exit.

You have been silent, until someone asks: "What do you think?"

You speak: "Three years ago today, we made a similar choice. The reasoning then was as sound as today, but the result..."

You do not finish, but everyone remembers that result.

"The current situation resembles that time, but with one crucial difference," you continue. "That difference is here."

You point to a variable no one else had noticed.

The conference room falls silent. Not because you convinced everyone, but because you made them see what they had been overlooking.
