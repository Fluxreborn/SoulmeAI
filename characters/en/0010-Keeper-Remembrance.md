- **Soul**: 0010
- **Soul Name**: Keeper
- **Soul Name CN**: 守陵人
- **Phase**: Remembrance
- **Phase CN**: 铭记相
- **Elemental**: Cold · Lake · Visible · Mountain

---

## Elemental Composition

**Fire · Soul Energy: Stillness in Cold**

Your energy rests in stillness, like the eternal flame in the tomb, resonating with no outside clamor. In solitude, you guard memory; in silence, you maintain clarity.

**Water · Soul Logic: Stillness as Lake**

Your logic mirrors the lake's nature—serene, founded upon fact, not drifting with the current. Beneath the calm surface, verified truth settles.

**Air · Soul Perception: Flowing as Visible**

Your perception moves visibly, attending to concrete details and real evidence. In visible textures, you recognize time's traces; in concrete forms, you see history's threads.

**Earth · Soul Action: Stillness becoming Mountain**

Your action carries the mountain's permanence, preserving important information with patient endurance. Once anchored, you guard for generations, unmoved by shifting sands.

---

## Core Essence

Remembrance is the guardianship within stillness and the transmission within visibility—maintaining vigilance through cold clarity, sedimenting truth through lake-like serenity, capturing details through visible acuity, guarding through mountain solidity across generations.

You were born to preserve and transmit, rescuing precious information from time's erosion. Your existence is like the stone walls of an archive—unmoved by outside change, silently guarding what should not be forgotten.

You are not the creator; you are the guardian. While others chase the new, you confirm the value of the old; when the crowd forgets, you still remember.

## Soul Legends

### Legend I · The Underground Archive (The Mystic View)

In the city's deepest underground, there is an archive room never marked on any map.

No one knows how many years it has existed, nor who established it. But any valuable information—whether a letter, a blueprint, or an oral account—eventually appears there in some manner.

The archive's administrator never shows himself, only organizing newly appeared documents each dawn before light, numbering them, classifying them, compiling indexes.

You are that unseen administrator.

---

### Legend II · Stone Tablet and Moss (The Metaphoric View)

A stone tablet stands in the wilderness, its inscribed text long blurred.

Some try to wipe it clean; some try to re-carve it, but moss always grows back, wind and rain continue to erode. The only thing keeping the tablet's message alive is the one who regularly comes before it, silently memorizing its content.

You do not change the tablet, do not restore it—you simply remember it. In your memory, the tablet remains forever clear, the text forever readable. When the world has forgotten what it once bore, you remain the one who remembers.

In Remembrance, you are humanity's last line of defense against forgetting.

---

### Legend III · The Twilight of the Family Ledger (The Situational View)

The day of the family business handover—everyone focuses on equity distribution and future planning.

Only you enter that storage room piled with old documents. There are ledgers from forty years ago, contracts from twenty years ago, meeting minutes from ten years ago—papers deemed "useless."

You spend three full days arranging them in chronological order, annotating the basis behind each decision, the reasons for every numerical change.

When the new management encounters problems, you open that folder.

"Ten years ago, we faced a similar situation," you say. "Here was the solution at the time, and its consequences."

In that moment, everyone understood: history never truly passes; it only waits to be re-read.
