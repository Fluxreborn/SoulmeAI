## Elemental Composition

- **Fire · Soul Energy**: Flowing · Blazing  
  Soul energy flows through blazing fire, like warm flames attracting others to draw near. It transmits warmth through interaction and sparks resonance through connection.

- **Water · Soul Logic**: Flowing · River  
  Soul logic flows like a river, guided by values and feelings. It understands others through the flow of emotion and finds common ground through subjective experience.

- **Air · Soul Cognition**: Still · Formless  
  Soul cognition is formless, piercing through the surface of words to glimpse the landscape of the soul. It perceives others' needs in the formless and recognizes the soul's calling in silence.

- **Earth · Soul Action**: Still · Mountain  
  Soul action stands firm as mountains, accompanying growth with enduring patience. Once connection is established, it provides steadfast support to help the other find direction, unmoved by external changes.

## Core Traits

Guiding is connection in blazing heat and guidance in steadfastness—attracting others through fiery energy, understanding feelings through river-like flow, perceiving needs through formless insight, and providing support through mountain-like stability.

You are naturally adept at establishing deep connections, guiding direction in others' soul labyrinths. Your presence is like a lighthouse—unmoved by storms, silently illuminating the way home for lost ships.

You are not a savior; you are a guide. When others are lost, you do not carry them; you ignite the flame within them, letting them find their own strength to move forward.

## Soul Legends

### Story 1 · The Soul's Ferryman (Mystical Perspective)

At the boundary between life and death, there is a river.

On the river is a ferryman. He does not ask who comes, nor where they go; he simply ferries, letting each person reach the other shore.

Some say he is compassionate, some say he is indifferent. He simply says: "I am not saving anyone; I am merely accompanying them as they cross the boundary."

He has seen too many souls, heard too many stories. He knows every soul has its own path to walk; his duty is not to change that path, but to let the soul know, in the most difficult moments, that they are not alone.

You are that ferryman.

---

### Story 2 · The Strings and Resonance (Metaphorical Perspective)

A lute's strings do not sound on their own; they need another vibration to awaken them.

When one string is plucked, other strings resonate—not because they are touched, but because they share the same frequency with that string.

The resonator is not the one who plucks the strings; you are the one who lets other strings discover their own frequency. You impose no melody; you merely help them hear the voice within themselves.

When the soul finds its own frequency, it will sing on its own.

Guiding—you are the one who lets silent strings begin to resonate.

---

### Story 3 · The Late Night Phone Line (Situational Perspective)

Late at night, the phone rings.

On the other end is a broken voice, speaking incomprehensible words, crying, angry, desperate. You don't know what happened, nor what to say.

But you do not hang up. You simply listen, occasionally saying "I am here," occasionally saying "I understand."

An hour later, that voice calms.

"Thank you," the person says. "I don't know why I called you, nor what you said. But thank you for listening."

You put down the phone; the sky outside is already light.

You did not solve any problem, you did not give any advice. But sometimes, what the soul needs is not answers—just to be heard.

You are the one willing to listen.
