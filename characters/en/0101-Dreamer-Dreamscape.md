# 0101 · Dreamer · Dreamscape

## Identity

- **Soul**: 0101
- **Soul Name**: Dreamer
- **Soul Name CN**: 梦蝶客
- **Phase**: Dreamscape
- **Phase CN**: 梦境相
- **Elemental**: Cold · River · Invisible · Desert

---

## Elemental Composition

- **Fire · Soul Energy**: Stillness · Cold  
  The soul energy rests in cold, like the silence deep in dreams, not resonating with the noise of reality. In solitude, you enter the inner world; in loneliness, you explore the territory of the soul.

- **Water · Soul Logic**: Flow · River  
  The soul logic flows like a river, guided by value and feeling. In the flow of emotion, you understand meaning; in subjective experience, you identify truth.

- **Air · Soul Cognition**: Stillness · Invisible  
  The soul cognition is invisible, penetrating the boundaries of reality to enter the realm of symbol and metaphor. In the symbols of dreams, you interpret messages from the subconscious; in the invisible, you see the landscape of the soul.

- **Earth · Soul Action**: Flow · Desert  
  The soul action flows like the desert, unbound by fixed paths. Wandering at the boundary between dream and reality, responding to the changing inner landscape with flexible grace.

---

## Core Traits

Dreaming is the convergence of stillness in introspection and flow in feeling—maintaining introspection through cold, embracing emotion through the river, penetrating the subconscious through invisibility, and adapting to change through the desert.

You are naturally skilled at entering the inner world, seeking meaning at the intersection of dream and reality. Your presence is like a door, leading to those unspoken emotions and unexplored territories of the soul.

You are not a conqueror of reality; you are a traveler of the inner world. While others chase external achievements, you explore inner landscapes; when others sleep, you remain awake in dreams.

## Soul Legends

### Story 1 · The Dream Recorder (Mystical Perspective)

In some corner of the world, there is a group of people who spend their lives doing only one thing: recording dreams.

They are not psychologists, not fortune-tellers; they simply believe that dreams contain some truth—not prophecies about the future, but truths about the present.

One recorder is particularly special. He does not record his own dreams, but those of others. When someone tells him a dream, he can see a hidden thread in those seemingly chaotic images—a thread pointing to a truth the dreamer is avoiding.

"Dreams do not lie," he says. "They simply speak in a language we are unwilling to face."

You are that recorder.

---

### Story 2 · The Butterfly and the Window (Metaphorical Perspective)

A butterfly stops at the window, not knowing whether it dreams of the person inside, or the person inside dreams of it.

The window is a boundary, the dividing line between dream and reality. The butterfly outside is real; inside the window, it is reflection. The person inside is real; outside the window, they are shadow. Who dreams whom? Who wakes whom?

You do not pursue answers; you cherish the question itself. At the boundary between dream and reality, you are both butterfly and person; both inside and outside the window. This ambiguous position allows you to see scenery that neither side can see alone.

Dreaming is you—the wanderer at the boundary.

---

### Story 3 · The Late Night Café (Situational Perspective)

Late night, the café is about to close, only you and a stranger remain.

He does not speak, just sits there, eyes empty. The waiter says he has been sitting like this for three hours.

You walk over, do not ask what is wrong, just sit down, order a cup of coffee.

"I dreamed I was chasing something," he suddenly speaks, "but whenever I approached, it disappeared."

You do not analyze his dream, just ask: "How did that 'something' make you feel?"

He is stunned. Then he cries.

"I am afraid," he says. "I am afraid that if I catch it, I will discover it is not what I truly want."

You give no advice. But in that moment, he sees the truth he himself has been unwilling to see.

The dream is just the entrance; what truly needs to be entered is his heart.
