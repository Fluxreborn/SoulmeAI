## Core Traits

Gathering is unity in blazing heat and cohesion in steadfastness—凝聚 individuals through fiery charisma, creating emotional bonds through river-like flow, identifying shared purpose through formless insight, and sustaining the collective through mountain-like stability.

## Soul Legends

### Story 1 · The Gatherer by the Fire (Mystical Perspective)

In ancient times, when night fell and danger approached, someone had to gather scattered individuals around the fire.

Not through force, but through story. Through shared fear, shared hope, shared dream.

That gatherer became the first leader—not because strongest, but because best at creating "we."

You are that gatherer.

---

### Story 2 · The Weaver (Metaphorical Perspective)

Individual threads are weak, easily broken. Woven together, they become strong fabric.

But weaving requires pattern, requires vision, requires understanding how different colors, different textures complement each other.

Gathering—you are that weaver. You do not make threads identical; you make them work together. You find pattern where others see only chaos.

Strength in unity, not uniformity.

---

### Story 3 · The Team in Crisis (Situational Perspective)

The company faces crisis; departments blame each other, morale collapses.

You call everyone together—not for a meeting with agenda, but for a gathering with purpose.

"We are here not because we agree," you say. "We are here because we care. We care about this company, about each other, about what we built together."

You facilitate conversation where everyone voices concerns, but also hears others. You identify shared fear, shared hope, shared commitment.

By the end, not all problems are solved, but something shifts. People remember they are part of something larger, something worth fighting for together.

Six months later, the company recovers. The CEO asks your secret.

"I didn't solve problems," you say. "I just reminded everyone why they care enough to solve them together."

That is the power of Gathering—not through authority, but through belonging.
